### Claim E1: "Our nation is back, bigger, better, richer and stronger than ever before."

**Rating:** Mixture

**Evidence:**
- U.S. GDP growth: 2.2% in 2025, down from 2.8% in 2024 (Biden's final year). GDP shrank in Q1 2025 for the first time in three years. ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/fact-check-trump-state-of-union-87b184f7a38e65fa97d27c05ba2497fa))
- Hiring: Employers added only 181,000 jobs in 2025 â€” the fewest outside a recession since 2002. ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/fact-check-trump-state-of-union-87b184f7a38e65fa97d27c05ba2497fa))
- Stock market: S&P 500 gained 17% in 2025, but was outperformed by South Korea (+71%), Hong Kong (+29%), Japan (+26%), Germany (+22%), UK (+21%). ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/fact-check-trump-state-of-union-87b184f7a38e65fa97d27c05ba2497fa))
- Trump's overall disapproval rating hit 60% in an ABC/WaPo/Ipsos poll released Feb. 22. Six in 10 Americans say the country is worse off than a year ago. ([ABC News](https://abcnews.com/Politics/fact-checking-trumps-state-union-address/story?id=130459365))

**Analysis:** Some economic indicators are positive (inflation down, crime down, Olympic gold). But U.S. economic growth was actually faster under Biden in 2024. Job growth is near-recessionary levels, the stock market underperformed peers, and a majority of Americans disagree with the assessment. "Stronger than ever before" is demonstrably false by most metrics.

### Claim E2: "In 12 months, my administration has driven core inflation to the lowest level in more than five years."

**Rating:** Mostly True

**Evidence:**
- Core CPI (excluding food and energy) was 2.5% YoY in January 2026, the lowest since April 2021 â€” approximately 5 years. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/trump-state-of-the-union-live-updates.html))
- Headline CPI was 2.4% in January 2026, down from 2.7% in December 2025. ([BLS via ABC News](https://abcnews.com/Politics/fact-checking-trumps-state-union-address/story?id=130459365))
- However, inflation was already 3% when Trump took office and had fallen from a 9% peak in summer 2022, a decline driven largely by Fed policy. "Plummeting" overstates the recent trajectory. ([KCRA/AP](https://www.kcra.com/article/fact-check-trump-state-of-union/70485471))
- Tariffs have pushed prices up on specific goods: ground coffee +34%, fresh produce and beef also up. Energy costs rose 6.3% from Jan 2025 to Jan 2026. ([ABC News](https://abcnews.com/Politics/fact-checking-trumps-state-union-address/story?id=130459365))

**Analysis:** The core inflation figure is technically accurate. But the word "plummeting" is an overstatement â€” the trend was already downward before Trump, and the Federal Reserve deserves significant credit. Tariff-driven price increases in several consumer categories undercut the broader affordability message.

### Claim E3: "Egg prices fell by 60%."

**Rating:** Mostly False

**Evidence:**
- Consumer Price Index data from the BLS report published February 13, 2026, shows egg prices down approximately **34.2%** year-over-year. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/13/egg-prices-falling-in-us.html))
- In January 2025, some retailers were charging over $9 for a dozen eggs during the avian flu outbreak. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/01/14/why-eggs-are-so-expensive-when-prices-will-drop.html))
- The 34.2% decline is significant but the actual figure is roughly 40% lower than Trump's stated 60%.

**Analysis:** Egg prices did fall substantially, primarily because the avian flu crisis that caused the spike has eased â€” not directly as a result of administration policy. The 60% figure overstates the decline by roughly 25 percentage points according to official government data.

### Claim E4: "As time goes by, I believe the tariffs, paid for by foreign countries, will substantially replace the modern-day system of income tax."

**Rating:** False

**Evidence:**
- Tariffs are legally paid by **U.S. importers**, not foreign countries â€” a basic economic fact repeated by virtually all economists. The burden then gets passed on to consumers through higher prices. ([CNN live blog](https://www.cnn.com/politics/live-news/trump-state-of-the-union-2026))
- The Supreme Court struck down the bulk of Trump's tariff authority (IEEPA-based) in a ruling just days before the SOTU. ([ABC News](https://abcnews.com/Politics/trump-continues-lash-supreme-court-after-tariff-ruling/story?id=130407379))
- The federal government collects approximately $30 billion/month in tariffs. Individual and corporate income taxes generate roughly $260 billion/month. Tariffs replacing income tax would require a ~9x increase. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/24/nx-s1-5716277/trump-state-union-fact-check))
- Manufacturing shed 108,000 jobs in 2025, the opposite of the renaissance Trump attributes to tariffs. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/24/nx-s1-5716277/trump-state-union-fact-check))

**Analysis:** Two claims embedded here are both false: (1) tariffs are not "paid by foreign countries" â€” they are paid by American importers and consumers; (2) the math for replacing income tax with tariffs doesn't work at current volumes, and the Supreme Court had just curtailed his tariff authority.

**Rating:** Mostly False

**Evidence:**

**Analysis:** Trump's own administration's website shows $9.6 trillion, less than half the $18T claimed. That figure is also inflated by including commitments made under the Biden administration and non-investment "economic exchanges." The $18T number appears to have no documentary basis.
